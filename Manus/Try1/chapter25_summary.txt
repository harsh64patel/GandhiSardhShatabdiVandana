"ઉપવાસ અને પ્રાર્થનાનું આધ્યાત્મિક મહત્વ"

This summarizes the main themes of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's spiritual understanding of fasting and prayer. The article begins with Gandhi's explanation that pure fasting purifies the body, mind, and soul - as the body suffers, the soul becomes free. He emphasizes that prayer without fasting is impossible, just as true fasting without prayer is impossible. Complete fasting represents complete self-control and is the truest form of prayer, requiring joyful surrender without calculation. The second section features Marathi writer V.S. Khandekar's reflection on how Gandhi transformed India's social service landscape after 1920. Khandekar describes how Gandhi added new dimensions of faith, duty, and sacrifice to public service, teaching that words without action have no value, action without sacrifice has no worth, and public work without dedication reaches nowhere. This inspired ordinary people to blossom and soar like birds measuring the sky. The article concludes with an anecdote where Gandhi explains the proverb "even a dead snake has its uses" through a story about an old woman who preserved a killed snake and used it medicinally. Gandhi extends this metaphor to suggest that we should learn from our enemies' criticisms and good qualities rather than simply destroying them.
