Based on the content of article 28 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -619" (which appears to be misnumbered in the blog), a suitable short title would be:

"આંબેડકર સાથે સમજૂતીના જોખમો"

This summarizes the main theme of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's concerns about reaching any agreement with Dr. Ambedkar. The article presents Gandhi's letter to Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel where he expresses deep reservations about negotiating with Dr. Ambedkar. Gandhi states that Ambedkar had explicitly told him that he makes no distinction between truth and falsehood or between violence and non-violence, being willing to adopt any means to achieve his desired ends. Gandhi warns that such a person, who might change religious affiliations as needed, requires extreme caution. He also expresses concern that despite any agreements made, injustice against Harijans would continue if power fell into the hands of zealots rather than those with moral principles. Gandhi notes that many Congress members themselves were guilty of discriminating against Harijans. The commentary by Ramesh Oza provides historical context, explaining that Ambedkar had recently suffered electoral defeat, partly due to Patel's opposition, and was attempting to ally with the Muslim League by offering support for Pakistan in exchange for 20% reservation, which alarmed Gandhi and other Congress leaders.
