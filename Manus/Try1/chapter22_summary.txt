Based on the content of article 22 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -22", a suitable short title would be:

"સાવધાનીથી ચાલવાનો મંત્ર"

This summarizes the main themes of the article, which begins with Gandhi's interpretation of a verse from Ramdas Swami's "Manache Shlok" about walking carefully and speaking thoughtfully. The verse advises maintaining a middle path - neither too excitable nor too rigid - and performing work so well that people don't even notice who did it. The second section features Marathi writer V.V. Shirwadkar (Kusumagraj) who proposes that respect for Gandhi is one of two fundamental measures of a person's humanity (the other being appreciation for Sharadchandra Chattopadhyay's novels). The article concludes with an anecdote about Gandhi at the Indian Opinion printing press, where he chose to manually turn the printing wheel himself rather than having donkeys whipped to do the work. Despite the physical strain that left him drenched in sweat, Gandhi maintained that this manual labor was their duty and described it as a sacred act (yajna) that taught the value of physical labor and cooperation.
