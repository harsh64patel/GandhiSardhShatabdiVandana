Based on the content of article 23 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -23" (though displayed as article 24 in the title), a suitable short title would be:

"ગામડાંને વસવા લાયક બનાવવાનો સંદેશ"

This summarizes the main theme of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's message to youth about living in villages and making them more livable. The article begins with Gandhi's advice to young people to settle in villages and make them more attractive and habitable through dedicated service to villagers. He suggests that by working diligently to make villages cleaner and removing ignorance, youth can create positive change, as their pure, organized, and industrious lives will inevitably influence the communities where they work. The second section features Marathi writer V.V. Shirwadkar's recollection of seeing Gandhi during the Quit India movement in 1942, comparing the experience to Arjuna's vision of Krishna's universal form in the Mahabharata. Shirwadkar describes how Gandhi had conquered the realms of thought, emotion, and action in India like Vamana's three steps. The article concludes with an anecdote about Krishnalal Mohanlal Zaveri, a scholar of Gujarati literature and Persian, who was invited by Gandhi to join his work but requested to wait until his retirement.
