Based on the content of article 26 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -639" (which appears to be misnumbered in the blog), a suitable short title would be:

"સરદાર પટેલની રાજકીય દૂરદર્શિતા"

This summarizes the main theme of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's insights about Sardar Patel's political wisdom and approach to minorities. The article begins with Gandhi addressing concerns about Sardar Patel's alleged intentions toward Muslims in India. Gandhi clarifies that while Patel had expressed doubts about the loyalty of some Muslims, he did not allow these suspicions to influence his official actions. Gandhi advises Muslims to stay in their homes even if threatened, facing death with courage rather than abandoning their homeland. He also suggests that Indian Muslims demonstrate their loyalty by advocating for the return of Hindu women abducted in Pakistan. The commentary by Ramesh Oza explains how Gandhi identified three key points: first, the paradox of a majority fearing a minority (which he found shameful); second, the principle of not abandoning one's homeland even under threat (advice he gave to Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan as well); and third, the political wisdom of Sardar Patel who, despite his suspicions, maintained fair governance. The article reveals both Gandhi's foresight and Sardar Patel's political acumen in handling the complex communal situation during India's partition.
