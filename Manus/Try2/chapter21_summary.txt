Based on the content of article 21 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -21", a suitable short title would be:

"કબીરના ભજન અને જીવનનો સમન્વય"

This summarizes the main themes of the article, which continues Gandhi's interpretation of Kabir's bhajan "Zhini Zhini Zhini Bini Chadariya" and explores the integration of spiritual and worldly life. The article begins with Gandhi explaining Kabir's teaching that we shouldn't be attached to our bodies since they are temporary like worn clothes, while also emphasizing that we must engage in productive work while alive. The second section by Manubhai Pancholi draws parallels between Gandhi and other spiritual leaders like Guru Nanak and Ramakrishna Paramahansa who taught the harmony of spiritual realization and worldly service. Pancholi notes that Gandhi, like these saints, didn't reject society but transformed it into an offering to the divine. The article concludes with a moving anecdote of Gandhi in Calcutta during the post-partition riots, where he wept while counseling a Muslim youth seeking revenge for his murdered family members to choose forgiveness over retaliation.
