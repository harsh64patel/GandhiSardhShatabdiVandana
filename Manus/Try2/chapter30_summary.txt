Based on the content of article 30 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -639" (which appears to be misnumbered in the blog), a suitable short title would be:

"સરદાર પટેલ અને મુસ્લિમ સમુદાય"

This summarizes the main theme of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's views about Sardar Patel's relationship with the Muslim community. The article presents Gandhi's clarification that Sardar Patel did not intend to drive Muslims out of India, despite having suspicions about the loyalty of some Muslims. Gandhi emphasizes that Patel did not allow his suspicions to affect his actions and decisions. The article also discusses Gandhi's advice to Muslims not to leave their homes under any circumstances, even if faced with violence, and his similar advice to Khan Abdul Ghaffar Khan to remain in Pakistan despite persecution. The commentary by Ramesh Oza explains how communal politics operates on fear, with both majority and minority communities being made to fear each other, and how Gandhi disliked false self-deception, false pride, and self-pity that are essential components of communal politics.
