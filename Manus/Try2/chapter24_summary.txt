Based on the content of article 24 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -24", a suitable short title would be:

"ઉપવાસ અને પ્રાર્થનાનું મહત્વ"

This summarizes the main themes of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's views on fasting and prayer. The article begins with Gandhi's explanation of fasting as an essential spiritual practice that has become an integral part of his nature - as necessary for inner vision as eyes are for external sight. He clarifies that his fasts are not undertaken for publicity or fame, but are prompted by divine power that also gives him strength to endure them. He emphasizes that fasts should never be undertaken in anger or on others' commands. The second section features Marathi writer V.S. Khandekar's anecdote about how Gandhi helped him appreciate his village Shirode as a "book" worth reading, transforming his perception from finding it boring to seeing it as a source of inspiration. The article concludes with an incident where Gandhi explains to Ruchi Adam that prayer is the soul of the ashram and those who object to prayer cannot become residents (though they may stay as guests). Gandhi elaborates on prayer being the nourishment, breath, strength, light, joy, peace, truth, duty, and ultimate surrender of the soul.
