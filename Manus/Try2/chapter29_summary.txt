Based on the content of article 29 "ગાંધી સાર્ધ શતાબ્દી વંદના -629" (which appears to be misnumbered in the blog), a suitable short title would be:

"ભારતના ભાગલા અને બ્રિટિશ નીતિ"

This summarizes the main theme of the article, which focuses on Gandhi's opposition to the partition of India and his views on British policy. The article presents Gandhi's conversation with a Reuters representative where he reiterates his opposition to partition based on religious lines. Gandhi argues that the British should leave India immediately rather than staying for another thirteen months, as their continued presence was causing more harm than good. He believed that the communal conflicts were partly due to the British presence, and that Indians had been trained to look to British authority for everything. Gandhi also mentions his meeting with Jinnah where he maintained his opposition to Pakistan while suggesting that Jinnah should convince his opponents through persuasion rather than force, consistent with his professed faith in non-violence. The commentary by Ramesh Oza explains how Gandhi, though opposed to partition, accepted it when it happened and advocated for friendly relations between India and Pakistan, emphasizing the protection of citizens' rights in both countries.
